About Me

My name is Danish Singh. I was born in Kampala, Uganda and lived there for a couple of year before my family moved to Canada when I was seven years old. I grew up in Guelph, Ontario. I attended Wilfrid Laurier University for Economics and a minor in Psychology. I later transferred into Architecture at Conestoga College. During this time I had started to learn how to code websites with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I came across game development and was instantly interested and keen to learn more. I started with simple games in Python, to a few platformers in Construct 2, to Unity and eventually, Unreal Engine. I also decided to complete a Game Development and Game Design course at Toronto Film School. Although I have had a difficult time finding an entry-level position to start my career in Game Development, I have spent the remainder of the time growing my skillset and delving into other creative processes such as 3D modeling and animation. I have experience with 3DS Max but I am mainly using Blender to create my art assets. Apart from growth in the field of game development, I am also an avid reader and have recently started to write my own pieces, fiction and non-fiction.

All in all, through my nature or sheer coincidence, I am and am further becoming a Jack-Of-All-Trades developer. Experience working in a collaborative studio with other developers and artists would only further develop my skillset.
